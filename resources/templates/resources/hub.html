Volunteer Guide

A Skywalk is an outreach event where Under One Sky teams walk the streets to connect with homeless friends, providing essentials like food, water, hot drinks, clothing, practical help, and companionship. Each event is led by an experienced Team Leader and focuses on building trust and friendship.

  • Individual Skywalks can serve anywhere between 20 and 100 people
  • Each group consists of up to 5–6 volunteers plus a Team Leader
  • We distribute sandwiches, soup, crisps, sweets, water, hot drinks, underwear, socks, clothing, toiletries, and SIM cards when available

Check in with yourself before joining a Skywalk. While it's rewarding, it can also be draining, especially if you're unwell or emotionally exhausted. The first walk can be particularly emotional, so give yourself space to process your feelings. If you're not in the right headspace, it's okay to take a break — supporting others starts with taking care of yourself.

Summer
  • Water bottle, sports drinks, high-energy snacks
  • Sunscreen, hat, sunglasses
  • Rain poncho (umbrellas are not ideal in crowds)
  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Antibacterial hand gel
Winter
  • Thermos with hot drinks, insulated water bottle
  • High-energy snacks
  • Extra warm layers, waterproof gloves
  • Waterproof winter walking boots
  • Rain poncho
  • Antibacterial hand gel

Volunteering with homeless friends is safe as long as you follow basic precautions and work in a team. Most of our friends are kind and grateful.

  • Treat everyone with respect — use their name if shared, and speak as equals
  • Listen with empathy and let them lead the conversation
  • Offer help, but don't force it — some may not want to engage
  • Respect their space; greet gently — "Would you like a hot drink or a snack?"
  • If they don't respond, don't push — let them know you're here
  • For sensitive topics: listen, acknowledge feelings, and refer to a service if appropriate
Photography: Do not photograph homeless friends without explicit consent. Consider photographing volunteers, supplies, or general scenes instead.

  • Keep personal details private — do not share your name, phone number, address, or social media
  • Do not make promises you can't keep
  • Do not give money or personal gifts — private donations must never be handed out by volunteers
  • If a follow-up is needed, obtain the person's contact details and pass to the Team Leader
  • Seek support from your Team Leader if a situation feels unsafe — stepping away is always a valid choice

If someone becomes upset or angry
  • Stay calm and listen actively
  • Acknowledge their feelings without escalating the situation
  • If it feels unsafe, step back and seek help from your Team Leader
If someone requires urgent assistance
  • Stay calm and assess the level of urgency
  • Do not provide medical assistance unless trained
  • Call 999 if necessary
  • Stay with the person (if safe) until help arrives
  • Notify your Team Leader — they will complete an Incident Form

  • Respond to Mikkel's broadcast message
  • Reply to the shout-out in your relevant WhatsApp group
  • To join a different route, text Mikkel to be added to that WhatsApp group
Team Leader Guide

Read this to the team at the start of every Skywalk.

Team
We leave as one and return as one. We respect and protect each other.
Boundaries
I make my boundaries clear. My team helps me enforce them.
Compassion
We are a safe space and always see the human being first.
Dignity
We make people feel seen, heard, valued and take action when needed.
Integrity
I treat my team and our friends as I would like to be treated.

  • Brief first-time Skywalkers on how we operate — values, practical process, and photography approval
  • Ensure supplies are readied and packed in the trolleys
  • Monitor the WhatsApp group for late arrivals or urgent updates
  • Introduce all team members by name
  • Read out the Skywalk Code
  • Make clear that any volunteer can stay in the background if uncomfortable serving a specific person
  • Inform the team of anyone on the "tracked list" who has exhibited inappropriate behaviour
  • Check-in score: ask each team member how they feel 1–5 (low to high). If anyone scores 1, have a private conversation before proceeding

  • Keep the group together and maintain a duty of care at all times
  • Communicate with all team members so everyone feels included
  • Pre-warn the team before approaching anyone on the watch list
  • Keep count of how many people you connected with and served
  • Ensure special requests are logged using the Special Requests form
  • Stay vigilant of your surroundings

  • Check-out score: repeat the 1–5 score with the team and note results
  • Check if anyone would like to raise something from the walk
  • Pack up all leftover supplies and return to storage (no food should be left accessible to vermin)
  • Submit the Skywalk Report form
  • Report any incidents within 24 hours via the Incident Form
  • Write a short summary and share in the WhatsApp group

Unpleasant interactions often stem from trauma or learned behaviours. Approach them with both firmness and empathy. Healthy boundaries are not the opposite of compassion — they create mutual respect.

Before the walk
  • Maintain a tracked list of individuals who have exhibited inappropriate behaviour and share it with the team
  • Remind volunteers about personal boundaries — comfort zones vary and each person decides their own
  • Normalise conversations about discomfort; use check-out discussions to surface any issues
During a difficult interaction
  • Volunteers may choose not to engage with anyone on the tracked list
  • If behaviour is inappropriate, let the individual know it has made team members uncomfortable
  • If it persists, explain that continued service may not be possible
  • For untracked individuals, volunteers should feel empowered to step away without hesitation
Homeless Friend Support

Where a homeless friend's wellbeing is at imminent and severe risk, the usual course of action is to arrange an emergency night in a hostel. Past examples include underage individuals, visibly pregnant women, people discharged prematurely from hospital, and people at risk of hypothermia.

The key judgement call is whether a hostel stay or calling emergency services is the right course of action.

For a second opinion or where a hostel booking is required, call Brian Whiting or Mikkel Juel Iversen on 07950 366 448.
Always list the situation in the Team Leader report.

Where a homeless friend has a wish and an opportunity for immediate change, we will attempt to provide support. Past examples include people who have managed to get into work but have not yet received their first pay cheque, people who have just become homeless, and people who have an opportunity to be housed by family or friends (in the UK or abroad).

Our action may be an offer of temporary hostel accommodation or sponsorship of travel.

The Team Leader should raise the situation with Brian, who will call or meet the person to understand the circumstances.
Always list the situation in the Team Leader report.
Sandwich Making

  • Volunteers and staff are responsible for sandwich preparation
  • Event Coordinators oversee quality and quantity
  • Sandwiches should be prepared and delivered to designated locations as per the Skywalk route. Deliveries can be arranged via Uber or by volunteers directly
  • Any delays or issues must be communicated promptly
Supplies needed

Ingredients: Bread (white, 50/50, whole-grain, or wholemeal), spreads (butter or margarine), fillings (ham, turkey, beef, egg mayo, cheese, etc.), and optional toppings.

Wrapping: Wax paper, parchment paper, plastic wrap, aluminium foil, or cling film.

Labelling: Waterproof markers or labels for clear identification.

Assembly
  1. Spread butter or margarine on one side of each bread slice
  2. Add fillings (cheese, ham, turkey, beef, egg mayo, or alternatives)
  3. Add optional toppings like pickles, tomatoes, or lettuce
Cutting

Cut sandwiches into two identical rectangles — not triangles. This gives equal portions, better stability, and easier wrapping and transportation.

Wrapping

Wax paper / plastic wrap: Fold sides over sandwich, fold the bottom edge up, secure with tape or sticker.

Aluminium foil / cling film: Fold sides over, then roll from the bottom up ensuring it's tightly wrapped.

  1. Organise sandwiches into bags by filling type (e.g. "Cheese", "Ham", "Vegan")
  2. Label clearly — filling type, dietary notes (e.g. "Vegan", "Gluten-Free"), and quantity. Use waterproof markers so labels stay legible in wet conditions
  3. Include a note with the total count and any special instructions
  4. Double-check contents before sealing
Clear labelling is especially important in winter when reduced daylight makes visibility more challenging.

We always aim to include vegan options. Ensure ingredients are certified or clearly labelled as suitable for vegans.

Vegan ingredients
  • Jam (check for vegan suitability)
  • Peanut butter (check for vegan suitability)
  • Vegan cheese alternatives
  • Tomatoes, cucumber, avocado, hummus
Classic vegan combinations
  • Hummus, cucumber, carrot, spinach, bell pepper on wholegrain
  • Mashed avocado and tomato
  • Peanut butter and banana on wholemeal

Common allergens: Nuts Dairy Eggs Gluten Fish & shellfish Soy Sesame

Preventing cross-contamination
  • Wash hands before and during preparation
  • Use separate utensils for each filling type
  • Clean surfaces between different fillings
  • Store ingredients in clearly labelled, separate containers

Personal hygiene
  • Wear suitable, clean, protective clothing
  • Keep hair tied back and wear a head covering (hat or hair net)
  • Do not wear watches or jewellery (except a plain wedding band)
  • Do not touch your face or hair, smoke, spit, sneeze, eat, or chew gum while handling food
Handwashing

Wash hands thoroughly before preparing food; after touching raw food; after handling waste or emptying a bin; after cleaning; after blowing your nose; after touching phones, switches, or door handles. Dry with a disposable towel.

Do NOT handle food if you are ill with a disease likely to be transmitted through food, have infected wounds or sores, or have diarrhoea.
Source: Food Standards Agency
Code of Conduct

The UOS Code of Conduct ensures everyone understands the standard of behaviour required. Volunteers commit to reading and agreeing to this Code and the Safeguarding Policy before signing up.

As a volunteer you commit to acting in the best interests of the team in order to collectively act in a safe, effective, and efficient manner.

  • Carry out duties responsibly, safely, and competently without being judgemental or biased
  • Avoid providing inaccurate information — inform the Team Leader to follow up with professional advice if needed
  • Handle UOS resources conscientiously; supplies are for homeless friends, not team members
  • Report any issues or concerns to your Team Leader, who will complete an Incident Report and escalate as appropriate

  • Respect people experiencing homelessness as individuals, regardless of their economic status, abilities, race, customs, culture, or religion
  • Treat all homeless friends equally with regards to communication and access to supplies
  • Be aware of the impact your actions may have on the environment and people around you

  • Behave in a manner that does not bring UOS into disrepute
  • Avoid actions that will bring harm to another person or property
  • Do not hand out private money donations while on UOS duty
  • Do not share personal details with homeless friends; pass any follow-up contact details to the Team Leader

Do
  • Represent UOS and its values at all times
  • Follow health, safety, and safeguarding guidelines
  • Maintain confidentiality and respect privacy
  • Practise self-care and communicate any limitations to your Team Leader
  • Report anything of potential threat or harm to your Team Leader immediately
  • Be supportive of your teammates
  • Be punctual and alert the Team Leader if you are late or unable to join
Don't
  • Proselytise (attempt to convert people to a religion)
  • Create dependency or overprotect homeless friends
  • Make empty promises or give material rewards without consulting the Team Leader
  • Bring or drink alcohol, or take drugs during volunteering
  • Verbally, emotionally, or physically abuse anyone
  • Upload photos of homeless friends to social media
  • Share information about homeless friends with anyone outside UOS
"When volunteering, please carry out the service with lots of love and compassion." — Always act in a way which upholds the reputation and values of Under One Sky.

All information about UOS — especially personal data of homeless friends — must not be disclosed or used for any purpose other than fulfilling your volunteering responsibilities.

Photographs and videos are considered personal data. Consent must be sought and the intended use must be clearly explained. Avoid asking those who have already declined.

All volunteers must comply with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). If in doubt, consult your Team Leader.

Safeguarding Policy

Under One Sky is committed to ensuring that everyone who comes into contact with our organisation is safe and safeguarded. Safeguarding is everyone's responsibility and is embedded throughout our culture.

This policy is not about creating barriers or bureaucracy. It is about being aware, compassionate, and responsive — knowing boundaries, escalating concerns appropriately, and ensuring accountability at all levels.

Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL)
Mikkel Juel Iversen

Main point of contact for concerns. Provides guidance and liaises with statutory agencies.

Deputy DSL
Julia Lalla-Maharajh

Supports the DSL and acts in their absence.

Safeguarding Trustee
Sara Carnegie

Board-level oversight, reviews complex cases.

Volunteers

Not expected to intervene or investigate — observe, listen, and escalate concerns to the DSL. Always act with dignity and follow the Code of Conduct.

All concerns must be taken seriously. Our response is guided by five principles:

Promptness
Act without delay
Transparency
Raise concerns openly
Respect
Treat all with dignity
Proportionality
Match response to risk
Accountability
Record decisions clearly

Report concerns promptly to the DSL. If the concern is about the DSL, escalate to the Safeguarding Trustee. If in doubt, record the concern.

While UOS's focus is on adults, we may encounter children at risk during outreach. In such cases, escalate concerns immediately to safeguarding leads or statutory services.

Young volunteers are welcome. All under-18s take part in age-appropriate activities only, with suitable supervision. Parental/guardian consent is required for under-16s.

Place-based settings (such as The Lighthouse) are limited to adults aged 18 and over.

About Under One Sky

The idea for Under One Sky came from founder Mikkel Juel Iversen's personal experience of supporting two homeless friends for a year each. At Christmas 2012, Mikkel and colleague Yaqub Hussain invited friends to walk the streets of Central London — distributing Christmas presents and letting those affected by homelessness know they hadn't been forgotten. This became the unintentional birth of Under One Sky.

From 2012 to 2019 the volunteer base grew from 8 to over 600 people. Covid-19 forced UOS from a winter pop-up to a 24/7 operation. During the height of the pandemic our teams supported 600 people daily, served our homeless friends 75,000 times in the first year, and the volunteer base grew to more than 1,000 people.

We believe humanity is one large connected family regardless of nationality, ethnicity, religion, age, gender, sexuality, or any other label.

Vision: A world without homelessness.
Values: Love, service, community, compassion, courage, and impact.
Mission: To use education, compassion and collaboration to end homelessness in the UK and beyond.

We call those we support our friends — to celebrate their individuality and highlight the equality in our relationships. The foundation of our work is the power of human connection.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I join different routes?

Yes! Simply text Mikkel to be added to the relevant WhatsApp group.

Can I bring my dog?

We kindly ask volunteers not to bring dogs. They can be distracting, and we operate in crowded areas where it may not always be appropriate.

How large are the groups?

Up to 5–6 volunteers plus a Team Leader per group. Individual Skywalks can serve between 20 and 100 people.

How can I help with preparations?

Trolleys need to be packed before walks — sorting clothes, stocking supplies, and organising food items. Help is always welcome.

Are there other volunteering opportunities?

Yes — UOS offers various roles including outreach and operational support. Reach out to find out more.

What do we hand out?

Sandwiches, soup, crisps, sweets, water, hot drinks, underwear, socks, clothing, toiletries, and SIM cards when available.

Why do we include sweets?
  • Quick source of energy for those experiencing hunger or exhaustion
  • Comforting and can lift morale
  • Many of our homeless friends specifically request them
Are wipes available?

Usually, but availability may vary due to high demand.

Do we provide SIM cards?

Yes, but availability is limited. Speak to the Team Leader to check current stock.

Can we accommodate special requests?

We try our best — our resources depend on donations and availability.

How is UOS funded?

Private and corporate donations. As a registered charity since May 2022, we also aim to secure funding from foundations.

How can the public donate?
  • Cash: Donation tin in the Team Leader rucksack
  • Online: QR code on the trolley or UOS business cards
How do I get UOS branded clothing?

Orders are placed once or twice a year in bulk. Watch out for announcements from Mikkel.
